Tomorrow at CESTA

Open House for Graduate Students:

"DH and Graduate Studies at Stanford and Beyond"
October 11 | 12-1.15 PM | Wallenberg Hall, Room 433A
About this event:  CESTA cordially invites graduate students to an open house, with lunch. All graduate students are welcome, irrespective of year, discipline, knowledge of the digital humanities, or technical expertise. Participants will learn more about CESTA’s support for the digital humanities and the Center’s unique opportunities for graduate students, including the Graduate Certificate in Digital Humanities and Digital Humanities Graduate Fellowship. Grads will have an opportunity to meet peers and raise questions with Mark Algee-Hewitt, Associate Professor of English and Director of the Graduate Certificate, Will Fenton, Associate Director of CESTA and Director of the Graduate Fellowship Program, and Alix Keener, Digital Scholarship Coordinator at CESTA.

About this series: Every Tuesday lunchtime (12-1.15 PM) during the academic quarter, CESTA hosts events on topics relevant to the digital humanities. For information about upcoming events in this series, visit our website.

News & Announcements

We are so pleased and proud to announce that Mark Algee-Hewitt has been awarded tenure and is now Associate Professor of Digital Humanities in the English Department, the first professor of Digital Humanities at Stanford! Mark is the director of the Literary Lab, where he oversees a wide range of research projects, and directs CESTA's Graduate Certificate in Digital Humanities. Congratulations, Mark!

Applications are now open for this year's
Digital Humanities Graduate Fellowship!


The fellowship, which is now in its eleventh year, will run from January to June 2023 and supports graduate students and postdoctoral fellows to produce publishable research using DH methods and approaches. The support of a research assistant will be available to all fellows.

Learn more about the program on our webpage or start your application now. Applications close at 11.59 PM Pacific Time, November 11 2022.

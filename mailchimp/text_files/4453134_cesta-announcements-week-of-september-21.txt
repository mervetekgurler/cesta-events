** This Week
------------------------------------------------------------

CESTA Seminar
Poetic Media Lab: A Digital Humanities Hub
Tuesday, Sept. 22 from 12 - 1:15 pm

Via Zoom. Registration required. (https://cesta.stanford.edu/events/poetic-media-lab-digital-humanities-hub)
Free & open to the public.

This will be the Poetic Media Lab’s (https://poeticmedia.stanford.edu/) most comprehensive presentation to date, a celebration of the variety of initiatives it sponsors and an opportunity to engage with other educators, creators and researchers.

CIDR Workshop
Intro to R for Humanities and Social Sciences
Tuesday, September 22, 1 - 3 pm

Via Zoom. Registration Required. (https://library.stanford.edu/events/introduction-r-digital-tools-and-methods-humanities-and-social-sciences-1)
Limited to Stanford affiliates.

This introductory workshop is geared towards those who would like to get started with R or need to brush up on their skills. You will learn how to use R, RStudio, how to create objects in R, how to work with different data types, how to read tabular data into R and how to subset tables.

Rumsey Map Center Event
Data Visualization and the Modern Imagination: Virtual Exhibition Opening
Friday, September 25, 2020 at 3:00 pm

Via Zoom. Registration required. (https://stanford.zoom.us/webinar/register/WN_WynKLLYcTXuX6gdHmp3kuQ)

The newest virtual exhibition from the David Rumsey Map Center includes charts, maps, and other graphic representations that helped expand our collective sense of reality. Guest curator RJ Andrews will tour the exhibition's themes of nature, time, and society.

** Student Opportunities
------------------------------------------------------------

For Stanford Graduate Students
Digital Humanities Graduate Research Fellowship

Submit an application by October 6. (https://cesta.stanford.edu/dhgf)

Through the course of this program, fellows work with leading experts in digital humanities and will be prepared to enter the job market and explore future careers in digital scholarship. This year’s fellowship will offer additional resources to help students make the most of a year in which most learning will take place online.

The application is open to Stanford graduate students working on individual projects or collaborative projects with faculty. Priority is given to PhD students in the arts and humanities who have completed their required coursework or are TGR.

For Stanford Students
Web Assistant Position

The Materia research unit in the DLCL is looking for an assistant (https://cesta.stanford.edu/news/opportunity-stanford-students)  who can implement some design and layout changes and content updates on the Materia website during fall quarter. The Materia site uses the Drupal-based Stanford Sites platform widely adopted by groups and departments on campus. No previous experience with Drupal is necessary, and training on Drupal is available as part of this position. Previous experience using HTML and CSS is preferred. For more info (https://cesta.stanford.edu/news/opportunity-stanford-students)

Today at 12 pm:
CESTA Seminar
Dr. Christina Hodge, Academic Curator and Collections Manager of the Stanford University Archaeology Collections (SUAC), will present Modeling Mesoamerica: Origins and Opportunity in a Digital Exhibit.
In the context of Stanford's policy concerning the COVID-19 pandemic, CESTA events are online for now. We look forward to seeing you at CESTA when it is safe to gather again.

About this event: This presentation is an exclusive preview companion piece to the newest virtual exhibition from the Stanford University Archaeology Collections, opening Friday, October 23 (https://archaeology.stanford.edu/events/modeling-mesoamerica-origins-originality-teaching-collection) . Modeling Mesoamerica is a collaborative project featuring artifacts from SUAC’s permanent collection, curated by Stanford students in Hodge’s spring 2020 course Museum Cultures. Digital production was assisted by student Mireille Vargas, an undergraduate research intern at the Center for Spatial and Textual Analysis (CESTA).

Christina J. Hodge is Academic Curator and Collections Manager of the Stanford University Archaeology Collections (SUAC) of the Stanford Archaeology Center (https://archaeology.stanford.edu/) . She is an interdisciplinary museum anthropologist and historical archaeologist working in critical museum and heritage studies.
